Project

GUILT

GUILT is a quiz that determines the user’s similarities with British convicts that are recorded in the SLQ data. To achieve this, the users will be taken through multiple scenarios where they have to choose between the options given. These options will eventually lead them to the British convict that matches them. These options are presented through interactive visuals where the users are presented with information on the British convicts, the event that lead to their convictions and the result of it.

My role in the project was a designer and coder. I designed the functionality and interactivity of the application. To deliver this, I utlise Adobe Illustrator to create the wireframe and eventually implementing it through CSS, JavaScript.

Individual Project

The prototype runs as a game where the user have to navigate their character through a mission. The user will be given limited information and have to utilize It to find where the character should travel to. This concept is chosen to present the data in a unique and interactive way about the overseas travel that the SLQ members went.

I came up with the idea by combining the ideas discussed in the first and second contact session. In the beginning, I had an idea of filtering through the Overseas Travel data through a game. Design Choices

Overall, I chose a range of green as the color of the application. Since the application is directed to be used on a mobile phone, I adapted my designs to the common mobile phone dimension. I decided to go with a very simple design by not presenting a lot of information on one page. The first image on the right is the character page and only shows the headshot of the character,their name and their position. Same goes with the second image that only presents the users with 2 clues to help their mission. The last image is the main interaction of the application showing the world map across three pages. Navigation

With the mobile-phone design in mind, I created buttons and navigations throughout the application that is distinct and simple. The first image on the left highlighted the tick button on top left and the choose button in the bottom of the page. The tick button will then show the sidebar navigation that is shown by the image in the middle. The choose button guides the users to the next page. I decided to have a universal tick button throughout so that the user can go back to previous pages and see the information they have got. The side bar navigation shows where the users are at the moment and what else do they have to accomplish. The image on the left highlights the country pop-up and the next button. The country pop-up is a subtle way of delivering the information and it stands out on the world map. While the next and previous button are presented throughout the application so that it is easier for the users to roam around.

Wireframes

The initial concept of GUILT is that user could explore around the interactive pages in the first point of view. I initially designed the first interaction with a hover ability as shown above. Designing the wireframe was a challenge for me as I have never done a landscape design before. I watched a couple videos on YouTube on how to do a landscape illustration on Adobe Illustration. These videos helped me a lot in making sure to emphasizes on the objects that I want the user to see. However, taking into account that the page is expected to be used on a mobile application lead us to getting into a 3D concpet for the application. Eventually, we change the interface of the interactionand the concept due to inputs from other students and the tutors.

We knew from the beginning that we wanted to have a page in the end of every interaction for the users to choose their options from. A main reason for this is to encourage the users to explore around the interaction pages first and then make their decisions. The set up of the options page is chosen this way to highlights the task that the user needs to get that. It centralize the options and focuses the users attention to the options. Later on, we found that this concept is the easiest way to compile all of the user's data under the username.

To create more texture and incorporated the color scheme, I used the paintbrush tool, the pencil tool and the shape tool. Creating this design have helped me explore more tools to use and organizing the layers to emphasize on what I want to show.
